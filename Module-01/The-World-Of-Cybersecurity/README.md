# Module 1: The World of Cybersecurity

## Overview
This module introduces the basics of cybersecurity and explains how personal data, identity, and online activity are protected from cyber threats.

---

## What is Cybersecurity
Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting systems, networks, and data from digital attacks or unauthorized access. It applies at personal, organizational, and government levels.

---

## Personal Data
Personal data is any information that can identify a person.

Offline identity includes real-world details such as name and address.  
Online identity includes usernames, profiles, and online activity.

---

## Online Identity
A safe online identity should not reveal personal information.

Avoid using:
- Full name
- Birth year
- Address or contact details

Use unique and secure usernames for different accounts.

---

## Types of Data
Personal data includes:
- Medical records
- Educational records
- Financial information

If exposed, this data can lead to identity theft or fraud.

---

## Data Sharing Risks
Once shared online, data can spread across multiple devices and servers. It becomes difficult to control or remove.

---

## Smart Devices
Smartphones, apps, and wearable devices collect and generate personal data, increasing privacy risks.

---

## Cyber Threats
Cybercriminals target:
- Money
- Login credentials
- Personal information

Common attacks include phishing and impersonation.

---

## Identity Theft
Identity theft occurs when someone uses personal data for fraud.

Examples include medical and financial identity theft.

---

## Data Collection
Your data is also collected by:
- Internet service providers
- Advertisers
- Search engines and social media
- Websites using cookies

---

## Conclusion
Cybersecurity is essential to protect personal information. Users should stay aware, limit data sharing, and use strong security practices.
